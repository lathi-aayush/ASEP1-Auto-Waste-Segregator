What We Do

We classify dry waste into two categories:

Recyclable Waste

  • Paper and Cardboard
  • Plastics (bottles, containers, etc.)
  • Metals (aluminum cans, tin, etc.)
  • Glass (bottles, jars, etc.)

What Happens Next?

Recyclable waste is collected and sent to trusted NGOs and companies that specialize in repurposing these materials.

Non-Recyclable Waste

  • Contaminated paper or plastic (e.g., greasy food wrappers)
  • Laminated packaging
  • Thermocol and other non-recyclable plastics

How Do We Handle It?

Non-recyclable waste is responsibly managed to minimize environmental impact through sustainable disposal practices.

Why It Matters

By segregating your waste, you contribute to:

  • Reducing pollution by preventing waste from ending up in landfills or oceans.
  • Supporting circular economies where materials are reused rather than discarded.
  • Helping NGOs and businesses turn waste into opportunities, empowering communities and industries.

Join Us in Building a Greener Future

Segregation is the first step toward sustainability. Let’s work together to ensure that what can be reused doesn’t go to waste.

Together, we can make a difference—one recyclable at a time.
